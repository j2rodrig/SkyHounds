Handling of Quotes

To help prevent visual confusion between the single bottom quote (‚) and the comma (,), outermost quotations may not begin with the single bottom quote. To help disambiguate the different uses of the ASCII single quote, outermost quotations may not begin with the ASCII single quote, and no attempt is made to match pairs of ASCII single quotes. Finnish and Swedish quotation styles (”…” and ’…’ and »…») are not supported, nor are reversed quotation styles (»…«). However, such users may substitute ASCII double quote marks for outermost quotations, and prevent pairing of inner quote marks by using character literals. (E.g., "…[’]…[’]…".) French-style thin spaces next to quote marks (e.g., « … » ) are understood as literal spaces. Attempting to distinguish between literal spaces and spaces used for mere presentational formatting within strings may create too many ambiguities. Single-angle quote marks are not supported, although they may be embedded in quotations and comments.

Quotes, like other structural elements, may span multiple lines.

Comments

Outside of a quote, a horizontal bar (―) indicates the start of a comment.
